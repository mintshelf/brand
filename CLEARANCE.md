# Preliminary clearance record

This file records non-lawyer screening performed on July 20, 2026. It is not a
legal opinion, comprehensive clearance search, or conclusion that the name or
mark is registrable.

## Executive finding

The selected icon has no exact conflict identified in the searches below, but
it belongs to a crowded four-leaf and botanical design category. The **Mint
Shelf name has a material federal conflict candidate** and should not be treated
as cleared without advice from a qualified trademark attorney.

## Word-mark screening

The USPTO Trademark Search system returned no result for the exact combined-mark
query `CM:"MINT SHELF"`. The expanded query `CM:(MINT AND SHELF)` returned one
record:

- **SHELFMINT**, U.S. serial 87575519, registration 5961971
- Status checked in TSDR: `LIVE/REGISTRATION/Issued and Active`
- Owner: Shelfmint, Inc., a Delaware corporation
- International Class 035, active services: providing and operating an online
  distribution marketplace for buying and selling goods between manufacturers
  and retailers, plus related advertising and publicity

The order of the two words is reversed, but the registered services substantially
overlap Mint Shelf's planned marketplace activity. That combination makes this
a serious likelihood-of-confusion candidate requiring professional analysis.

General web screening also found historical and recent references describing
Shelfmint as a B2B food-and-beverage distribution marketplace. An exact-name
`mintshelf.nl` Shopify store for manga and anime was also indexed in 2026. Its
commercial status and any rights require verification. These findings are not
exhaustive common-law or international searches.

## Design-mark screening

The production icon in [`assets/marks/mark-green.svg`](assets/marks/mark-green.svg)
directly preserves independently generated concept C; it does not use the
retired Atlas or Logoipsum geometry.

Screening included descriptive web and image searches for four-part, four-leaf,
modular, rounded, botanical, cross-arranged, and green marks. Related visual
ideas are common in software, wellness, professional services, and botanical
brands.

Federal design searches included:

- Design code `05.03.07` (four-leaf clover): 2,187 live and dead records before
  narrowing.
- `DC:050307 AND GS:/.*marketplace.*/`: nine records. The live marketplace-related
  designs reviewed were not visually close to the selected Mint Shelf contours.
- Design-description searches combining four leaves or petals with marketplace
  services: 50 records before individual review and status analysis.
- Broader single-leaf code `05.03.25` combined with marketplace wording: 214
  records, demonstrating the category's density.

No exact federal design match was identified. The icon differs through its open
cross, unequal leaf sizes, organic contour differences, and slightly taller-than-wide
silhouette. Those differences do not establish registrability. The USPTO search
system's authenticated reverse-image feature was not used, and unindexed,
pending, foreign, state, and common-law designs may exist.

## Recorded adoption decision

On July 20, 2026, the founders decided to proceed with the Mint Shelf name and
selected design despite the unresolved SHELFMINT risk described above. This is
an explicit business-risk decision, not a conclusion that the name is legally
cleared. The decision should be revisited if Mint Shelf receives an objection,
files a federal application, materially expands its marketplace services, or
obtains new evidence of current SHELFMINT use.

## Recommended professional review

Before filing, launching broadly, or making an expensive downstream migration,
obtain a U.S. trademark attorney's assessment covering at least:

1. The live SHELFMINT registration and present commercial use.
2. The Mint Shelf word mark, spelling variants, reversed terms, and related
   commercial impressions.
3. The selected design mark using USPTO image search and appropriate design
   codes.
4. Relevant goods and services, likely including marketplace, downloadable
   application, and software-service categories.
5. Federal, state, common-law, domain, app-store, and relevant international
   use.

Proceeding before that review does not prevent Mint Shelf from seeking advice
later, responding to an objection, or reevaluating the name as the facts change.
